# Messaging & Positioning Analysis

## Goal
Decode how a competitor positions itself: the category they claim, their core value propositions, their messaging hierarchy, and the words they own in the buyer's mind.

## Best for
Sharpening your own positioning, writing differentiated copy, spotting an unclaimed angle, and preparing for head-to-head deals.

## Prompt
```text
You are a positioning strategist in the tradition of April Dunford. Analyze the messaging and positioning of {competitor}.

Examine their homepage, key product pages, and any "why us" / "vs" / about pages. Then extract:

1. CATEGORY: What category do they place themselves in? (The frame they want buyers to use.)
2. HERO PROMISE: The single headline message above the fold — verbatim — and what job it implies.
3. VALUE PROPS: Their 3-5 core value propositions, in their own words.
4. PROOF: What proof do they lean on? (logos, numbers, testimonials, awards, integrations, security)
5. TARGET CUSTOMER: Who is this clearly written for? Who is it NOT for?
6. TONE & VOCABULARY: The distinctive words and phrases they repeat. What terms do they try to "own"?
7. WEAKNESSES: Where is the messaging vague, generic, or making a claim a challenger could puncture?

Then summarize their positioning in one sentence using the format:
"For [target] who [need], {competitor} is a [category] that [key benefit], unlike [alternative]."

Quote real phrases from their site. Mark anything you infer vs. observe.

Competitor: {competitor}
URL: {competitor_url}
```

## Output format
- **Positioning statement** — the one-sentence summary in the template above.
- **Category & hero promise** — verbatim, with interpretation.
- **Value proposition stack** — ranked, in their words.
- **Proof points** — what evidence they use to back claims.
- **Target buyer** — who it's for and who it excludes.
- **Owned vocabulary** — the terms they repeat and want associated with them.
- **Soft spots** — generic or vulnerable claims you can position against.

## Optional inputs
- Your own homepage, for a side-by-side contrast.
- Their LinkedIn/ads copy for a fuller picture (see [LinkedIn monitoring](../social/linkedin-monitoring.md)).
- A specific competing deal or persona to focus the read.

## Notes
The goal isn't to copy their messaging — it's to find the angle they've *left open*. If three competitors all claim "the all-in-one platform," the winning move is usually the opposite. Combine with [competitive positioning](../sales/competitive-positioning.md) to turn the findings into a sales-ready contrast.
