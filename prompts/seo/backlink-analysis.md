# Backlink & Digital-PR Analysis

## Goal
Analyze a competitor's backlink and digital-PR strategy — where their authority comes from, what link-earning plays they run, and which of those you could replicate.

## Best for
Off-page SEO planning, digital-PR strategy, and understanding why a competitor outranks you despite similar content.

## Prompt
```text
You are an off-page SEO and digital-PR analyst studying how {competitor} earns its authority and backlinks.

Analyze (from their content, press, and any link data provided):

1. LINK-EARNING PLAYS: What tactics do they appear to use? (original research/data studies, free tools, integrations/partner pages, guest posts, PR/news, directories, community, comparison pages, scholarships, etc.)
2. LINKABLE ASSETS: The specific pages or assets that attract links — what makes them link-worthy?
3. AUTHORITY SOURCES: The types of sites likely linking to them (press, industry blogs, partners, aggregators). Note any flagship placements.
4. ANCHOR & TOPIC FOCUS: What topics and anchors their link profile seems built around.
5. PR & NARRATIVE: Are they running data-driven PR, thought leadership, or product-launch PR? What's the recurring narrative?
6. REPLICABLE PLAYS: The 3-5 tactics {your_company} could realistically copy or beat, ranked by effort vs. payoff.

Rules: Without a backlink tool this is partly inference from on-site signals — be explicit about that. If link data is pasted in, use it. Don't fabricate domain ratings or link counts.

Competitor: {competitor}
URL: {competitor_url}
Link data (optional): {paste_backlink_export}
```

## Output format
- **Link-earning plays** — the tactics in use, with evidence.
- **Linkable assets** — the pages doing the work and why.
- **Authority sources** — who's likely linking and any standout placements.
- **PR narrative** — the story they're pitching.
- **Replicable plays** — ranked by effort vs. payoff for you.
- **Caveats** — what needs a backlink tool to confirm.

## Optional inputs
- A backlink export (Ahrefs/Semrush/Moz) pasted in for real data.
- Your own linkable assets, to compare.
- A target publication list you're already pursuing.

## Notes
Without link-index data this is inference — say so, and validate the highest-value findings with a real backlink tool. The most actionable output is the "replicable plays" list: focus on the link-earning assets (data studies, free tools) that compound, not one-off placements. Their best linkable asset is often a clue to an [content gap](content-gap.md) you can fill better.
