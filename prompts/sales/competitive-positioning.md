# Competitive Positioning

## Goal
Position your product clearly and honestly against a specific competitor — a differentiation narrative reps and marketing can use consistently.

## Best for
Head-to-head deals, "vs." pages, and aligning the whole team on how you're different (not just "better").

## Prompt
```text
You are a positioning strategist. Build a clear competitive positioning of {your_company} against {competitor} for {target_buyer} in the {market} market.

Develop:
1. THE REAL DIFFERENCE: Not a feature list — the fundamental difference in approach, philosophy, or design. What do we believe about this problem that {competitor} doesn't?
2. WHO EACH IS FOR: The buyer profile {your_company} fits best, and the one {competitor} fits best. (Conceding a segment makes you more credible, not less.)
3. DIMENSIONS OF DIFFERENCE: The 3-5 axes where we genuinely differ (e.g., simplicity vs. configurability, breadth vs. depth, self-serve vs. white-glove). For each, where each product sits and why a buyer would prefer one.
4. THE NARRATIVE: A short, repeatable story that frames the choice in terms favorable to us — without lying about {competitor}.
5. PROOF: The evidence that makes the differentiation believable.
6. WHEN TO WALK: The deals where {competitor} is the right answer and we should qualify out.

Rules: Differentiate on something real and hard to copy, not a feature they'll ship next quarter. Be honest about their strengths — credible positioning acknowledges them. Avoid superlatives without proof.

Competitor: {competitor}
Target buyer: {target_buyer}
Market: {market}
Our positioning (optional): {your_positioning}
```

## Output format
- **The real difference** — the core philosophical/approach gap.
- **Who each is for** — honest segment fit for both.
- **Dimensions of difference** — the 3-5 axes, with where each sits.
- **Positioning narrative** — the short, repeatable story.
- **Proof points** — what makes it believable.
- **When to walk away** — deals to concede gracefully.

## Optional inputs
- Your current positioning and messaging.
- A [messaging analysis](../competitor-analysis/messaging-analysis.md) of the competitor.
- Win/loss patterns for which differences actually close deals.

## Notes
Position on something durable — a difference in philosophy or design that the competitor can't simply copy with a feature release. The willingness to say "they're the better fit if you need X" is what makes the rest of your positioning believable. Turn this into deal tactics with the [battlecard](../competitor-analysis/battlecard.md) and [sales playbook](sales-playbook.md).
