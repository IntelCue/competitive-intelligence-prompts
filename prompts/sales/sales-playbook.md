# Competitive Sales Playbook

## Goal
Build a competitive sales playbook for winning a specific segment or deal type — the end-to-end plan that ties positioning, discovery, objection handling, and proof into a repeatable motion.

## Best for
Sales leaders standardizing how the team competes, and reps preparing for a specific deal profile.

## Prompt
```text
You are a sales strategist building a competitive sales playbook for {your_company} to win {deal_profile} deals against {competitor} in the {market} market, selling to {target_buyer}.

Assemble a complete, practical playbook:

1. IDEAL DEAL PROFILE: The deals in this segment we're best positioned to win (and the ones to avoid).
2. DISCOVERY: The 6-8 discovery questions that surface the pain we solve better than {competitor} — and that quietly expose their weaknesses.
3. QUALIFICATION: The signals that this is a winnable deal vs. a {competitor} stronghold we should deprioritize.
4. POSITIONING & PITCH: How to frame {your_company} for this buyer, leading with the differentiation that matters to them.
5. DEMO STRATEGY: Which capabilities to show, in what order, to land the differentiation (and what to avoid showing).
6. OBJECTION HANDLING: The top objections in {competitor}'s favor and crisp responses.
7. PROOF & MOMENTUM: The case studies, ROI framing, and next-step structure to keep the deal moving.
8. CLOSE: Common stall points and how to advance them.

Rules: Make it tactical and rep-usable, not theoretical. Ground everything in real, honest differentiation. Note where a rep should qualify out rather than force-fit.

Competitor: {competitor}
Deal profile: {deal_profile}
Target buyer: {target_buyer}
Market: {market}
```

## Output format
- **Ideal deal profile** — where we win, where to walk.
- **Discovery questions** — 6-8 that surface our advantage.
- **Qualification signals** — winnable vs. avoid.
- **Positioning & pitch** — the frame for this buyer.
- **Demo strategy** — what to show, in what order.
- **Objection handling** — top objections and responses.
- **Proof & next steps** — momentum mechanics.
- **Close** — stalls and how to advance.

## Optional inputs
- Your existing case studies and ROI data.
- Win/loss patterns for this segment (see [win/loss analysis](win-loss-analysis.md)).
- A [competitive positioning](competitive-positioning.md) doc to anchor the pitch.

## Notes
A playbook is only useful if reps actually run it — keep it tactical, short, and specific to the deal profile rather than a generic "how to sell" doc. It's the assembly point for the other sales prompts: pull the pitch from [competitive positioning](competitive-positioning.md), the responses from [objection analysis](objection-analysis.md), and the quick reference from the [battlecard](../competitor-analysis/battlecard.md).
