# Competitive Objection Analysis

## Goal
Anticipate the objections a prospect will raise in favor of a competitor — and arm reps with honest, confident responses to each.

## Best for
Sales enablement, deal prep, and tightening responses to "but {competitor} does X."

## Prompt
```text
You are a sales enablement strategist. Build a competitive objection-handling guide for {your_company} reps facing {competitor} in deals with {target_buyer}.

For the {market} category, anticipate the objections a prospect leaning toward {competitor} will raise. For each:

1. OBJECTION: The objection in the buyer's own likely words.
2. WHAT'S BEHIND IT: The real concern or {competitor} talking point driving it.
3. IS IT FAIR?: Honestly — is the objection valid, partly valid, or a misconception? (Reps need to know.)
4. RESPONSE: A crisp, truthful reframe. Acknowledge what's true, then pivot to your strength. No spin that a smart buyer will see through.
5. PROOF: The evidence, story, or demo moment that backs the response.
6. TRAP: What NOT to say in reply (the response that escalates a feature war you'll lose).

Cover objections across: price, features, market presence/brand, integrations, risk/switching cost, and "we already use {competitor}."

Rules: Be honest where the competitor is genuinely better — reps lose deals by over-claiming. Ground responses in real differentiators. Keep each response short enough to say out loud.

Competitor: {competitor}
Target buyer: {target_buyer}
Market: {market}
Our differentiators (optional): {your_strengths}
```

## Output format
- **Objection cards** — for each: objection → what's behind it → fairness → response → proof → trap.
- **Grouped by theme** — price, features, brand, integrations, risk, incumbency.
- **Honest losses** — objections where the competitor genuinely wins, and how to qualify out gracefully.
- **Top 3** — the objections reps will hit most, flagged.

## Optional inputs
- Real objections from recent calls or your CRM.
- Your verified differentiators and proof points.
- The deal stage or persona to focus on.

## Notes
The fastest way to lose trust is to argue an objection that's actually true — the "is it fair?" and "honest losses" sections keep reps credible. Pull real objections from [win/loss analysis](win-loss-analysis.md) for accuracy, and roll the responses into the [battlecard](../competitor-analysis/battlecard.md) and [sales playbook](sales-playbook.md).
